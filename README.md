## What This Does

The classical Doppler formula is the first-order term ($n = 1$) of a Taylor expansion of the phase field along an observer's trajectory on a bounded phase manifold. This paper establishes the Taylor-Doppler identity: the Doppler effect, relativistic time dilation, and torsion are not separate physical phenomena but the same expansion evaluated at different shell orders.

The derivation does not invoke the postulates of special relativity or predefined coordinate transformations. The math stands on its own.

---

## The Core Derivation Chain

1. The phase field $\Phi(x,t)$ is expanded as a Taylor series in space
2. The observer trajectory $x(t) = x_0 + vt + \frac{1}{2}at^2 + \frac{1}{6}jt^3 + \cdots$ is substituted
3. The total time derivative $\omega = d\Phi/dt$ yields the observed frequency
4. Each derivative order maps to a harmonic shell:
   - $n = 0$: Rest frequency
   - $n = 1$: Classical Doppler
   - $n = 2$: Relativistic Doppler / Acceleration
   - $n = 3$: Torsion / Higher-order phase curvature
   - $n \geq 4$: Higher-order corrections
5. The Boundary Projection Operator $\hat{G}$ connects continuous phase expansion to discrete shell transitions
6. Numerical verification confirms the framework matches classical kinematics exactly at $n = 1, 2,$ and $3$
